Recipe

  1. Stir together garlic and soy sauce in a large bowl. Add chicken, onion and green onions. Toss to coat evenly.
  2. Marinate chicken thighs 30 minutes, or refrigerate overnight.
  3. Prepare a grill outside, or heat a grill pan on the stove. Coat thinly with oil, then add chicken and onions. Cook until done at the center, about 10 minutes each side.

Ingredients

1 clove of garlic (minced)

1/2 tbsp. soy sauce

1 chicken thigh (~ 1 lb or 0.45 kg)

1/4 onion (sliced thinly)

1/2 green onions (sliced)

1/4 tablespoon vegetable oil

Mona Emman Chicken

Mona Emman Chicken is a flavorful dish from the Marshall Islands that reflects the region’s rich culinary heritage. Its name, which translates to “Eat Fresh,” highlights the emphasis on using fresh, locally sourced ingredients. The dish features chicken marinated in a blend of soy sauce, garlic, and onions, then grilled to perfection and often served with rice or vegetables, creating a wholesome, satisfying meal. Rooted in the Marshall Islands’ tropical climate and abundant resources, the cuisine traditionally incorporates breadfruit, taro, coconut, and seafood, while imported ingredients like soy sauce and rice have been embraced over time, resulting in a unique fusion of flavors. Beyond its delicious taste, Mona Emman Chicken provides a balanced source of protein and essential nutrients from both the chicken and accompanying vegetables, supporting digestion, energy, and overall wellness. It is a true reflection of Marshallese culture, fresh ingredients, and wholesome cooking.
