Recipe

To get soft puttu, the amount of rice flour to water ratio is important. To check this gently press a small portion of the flour between your palms.
It should form a lump. When you press this flour lump more, then it breaks and crumbles. This should be the texture of the flour.

  1. Take 1 cup puttu flour in a mixing bowl or a pan.
  2. Add 1/3 teaspoon salt or as required.
  3. Now sprinkle 1/3 cup water all over. The amount of water required will depend on the quality of rice flour. So you can add accordingly.
  4. Begin to mix the rice flour with the water with your fingertips.
  5. Break all the tiny lumps in the flour with your fingertips.
    You can even grind this rice flour mixture in a grinder to break lumps. But grind in small batches and use the pulse option of mixer-grinder.
  6. Now pour 2 cups water in the base vessel of the puttu kudam.
  7. Keep on stove top and let the water get heated.
  8. Meanwhile first place the perforated disc inside the cylindrical vessel. Then add 2 to 3 tablespoons fresh coconut in the cylindrical vessel of the puttu kudam and spread evenly.
  9. Then gently add the puttu flour till it reaches half of the cylindrical vessel.
  10. Again add 2 to 3 tablespoons of grated coconut and spread evenly.
  11. Then add the puttu flour again.
  12. Cover the top portion with 2 to 3 tablespoons of grated coconut and spread evenly. Cover with the top lid.
  13. Place the cylindrical portion on top of the base vessel in which the water must have come to a boil.
  14. Steam puttu on medium flame till you see steam releasing from the top vents of the cylindrical vessel. Then turn off the the heat.
  15. Remove carefully with the help of a kitchen towel, the cylindrical part from the bottom vessel and allow the puttu inside to cool for 3 to 4 minutes.
  16. Then using a wooden skewer or the stick that accompanied the puttu kudam, remove the steamed puttu.
  17. Serve puttu hot or warm.

Ingredients

1 cup puttu flour

1/3 cup water (add more if/as required)

1/3 teaspoon salt (add more if/as required)

1/2 cup coconut

2-3 cups water (for steaming)

Puttu

Puttu is a traditional South Indian and Sri Lankan breakfast dish that has been savored for generations. Its name, meaning “portioned” in Malayalam, reflects the way it is steamed in cylindrical molds to create soft, layered cylinders of ground rice and coconut. Historically, Puttu has been a staple among coastal communities in Kerala and Tamil Nadu, often paired with lentil curries, bananas, or spicy chickpea stews. Its preparation highlights simplicity and nutrition, using minimal ingredients while retaining rich flavors and textures. Puttu is not only delicious but also highly nutritious: the rice provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy, while the coconut adds healthy fats and fiber to aid digestion. Often accompanied by protein-rich side dishes like chickpeas or lentils, it forms a balanced meal that supports overall wellness. Light, wholesome, and versatile, Puttu continues to be a cherished breakfast that combines heritage, taste, and health in every bite.
