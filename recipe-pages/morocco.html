Recipe

  1. Preheat your grill or chargrilled pan to high.
  2. Place the green peppers on your grill and turn occasionally every 3 to 4 minutes until each side is scorched (lightly burned) and tender.
  3. Remove the green peppers from the grill and set aside to cool.
  4. Meanwhile, peel, deseed, and roughly chop the tomatoes into 1.5 cm (1 inch) pieces.
  5. Once the green peppers have reached room temperature, peel and deseed and chop them into 1.5 cm (1 inch) pieces.
  6. In a large casserole or a deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-low heat and add the chopped tomatoes, garlic, spices, herbs, salt and sugar. Cover with a lid for 10 minutes until the tomatoes have softened. Stir occasionally.
  7. Uncover, add the chopped green peppers and mix all the ingredients together. Leave to simmer gently for 10 minutes until all the liquids evaporate.

Ingredients

2 large sweet green peppers

3 tbsp. olive oil

4 large tomatoes

1 tsp. (~2 cloves) finely chopped garlic

4 tbsp. mixture of fresh chopped parsley and coriander

1 tsp. paprika

1/2 tsp. cumin

Pinch cayenne pepper

1/2 tsp. salt or more to taste

1/2 tsp. caster sugar

Taktouka

Moroccan Taktouka is a warm, zesty salad that beautifully showcases the flavors of North Africa. Originating in Morocco, this dish is traditionally made by gently cooking tomatoes, green peppers, garlic, and olive oil until they meld into a vibrant, smoky mixture. Often served with crusty bread, Taktouka reflects Morocco’s love for shared, flavorful meals and is a staple in family gatherings and festive occasions. Historically, it emerged as a simple way to highlight seasonal produce while embracing the rich spice traditions of Moroccan cuisine. Beyond its irresistible taste, Taktouka is packed with health benefits: tomatoes and peppers are rich in antioxidants like vitamin C and lycopene, while olive oil adds heart-healthy fats. Low in calories yet high in fiber and nutrients, it supports digestion and overall wellness. Taktouka is more than a side dish—it’s a nourishing expression of Moroccan hospitality and culinary heritage.
