Recipe

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and saute for about 3 minutes. Add in garlic and cook another minute.
  2. Add oats and stir to combine. Pour in water and stir. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring to a boil. Decrease the heat to low and simmer for about 3 minutes. (The longer it simmers the thicker it will become, so cook them to the thickness of your liking.)
  3. Stir in the grated cheese and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add the spinach and stir until wilted. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil.

    5. You can serve with 4 fried/poached/soft-boiled eggs, black olives, sun-dried tomatoes, chili flakes, chili oil, or hot sauce. Here, I used eggs and chili flakes.

  5. Separate into 2 bowls and top with eggs and chili flakes. Enjoy!

Ingredients

1 teaspoon of avocado/olive oil

1/2 onion (finely chopped)

2 garlic cloves (minced)

1 cup rolled oats

2 cups water

1 tablespoon fresh or 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary (chopped)

1-2 cups spinach

Salt and pepper

Breakfast Oats

Oats have been used by humans for thousands of years. The oldest traces of oats being used by humans was found in a Southeastern Italian cave called “Grotta Paglicci”, which was used by hunter-gatherers more than 30,000 years ago. These traces could indicate that humans consumed oatmeal as early as 32,000 years ago. The first time humans consumed oatmeal (confirmed!) was around 3,000 years ago. Ever since, it has become a staple of almost every culture’s diet. Oatmeal is also extremely healthy and nutritious: it contains high levels of minerals like manganese, phosphorus, and magnesium, and vitamins like Vitamin B1 and B5; they’re rich in antioxidants; and they have beta-glucan fiber, which improves digestion and lowers cholesterol. In this recipe, you’ll make delicious oatmeal from the homeland of oatmeal: Italy.
Quick note: You can use fresh/dried thyme instead of rosemary; aged cheddar or feta cheese instead of Pecorino/Parmesan cheese; and kale/other leafy greens instead of spinach.
