Recipe

If you want to reduce the pungency of the red onion, you can soak it in water for 10-15 minutes. After soaking, rinse under cold water and pat dry before adding to the salad. Additionally, if you don't want the salad to be overly salty, don't add salt to the dressing, as the feta is already salty.

  1. Halve a cucumber lengthwise and slice it into half-moons. No need to peel or seed them.
  2. Slice the red onion thinly.
  3. Cut the feta into large cubes.
  4. Cut the tomatoes and olives into halves.
  5. Combine all vegetables into a bowl.
  6. Combine lemon juice with vinegar, and then gradually whisk in the extra-virgin olive oil, until the dressing is slightly thickened and completely homogenous.
  7. Your Greek salad is ready! Enjoy.

Ingredients

1 cucumber

1/2 red onion

1 pint (~568 mL) of grape/cherry tomatoes

6 ounces of feta, cut into 1/2 inch (~1.3 cm) cubes

2 tbsp. red wine vinegar

Juice of 1/2 lemon

1 tsp. dried oregano

Kosher salt

Freshly ground black pepper

1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Greek Salad

Greek Salad, or Horiatiki, is a vibrant dish that captures the essence of Mediterranean cuisine. Rooted in the rural traditions of Greece, it emerged as a farmer’s meal, made with fresh, seasonal ingredients straight from the land—juicy tomatoes, crisp cucumbers, briny olives, and creamy feta, all drizzled with golden olive oil. Beyond its refreshing taste, Greek Salad carries centuries of cultural heritage, symbolizing the simplicity and balance of the Greek diet. Today, it’s celebrated worldwide not just for its bold flavors but also for its health benefits. Packed with antioxidants, heart-healthy fats, vitamins, and fiber, it embodies the principles of the Mediterranean diet—linked to longevity and reduced risk of chronic diseases. Light yet nourishing, Greek Salad is more than a side dish; it’s a wholesome expression of Greece’s philosophy of eating well and living well.
